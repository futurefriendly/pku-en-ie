President Xi urges sound values for Chinese youth BEIJING, May 4 Chinese President Xi Jinping has urged the country's young people to study diligently, improve moral character, and hone their perspectives and honesty.
Xi made the remarks while visiting the prestigious Peking University on Sunday, China's Youth Day.
Xi extended greetings to Chinese young people of all ethnic groups on behalf of the Communist Party of China (CPC) Central Committee and stressed that they must follow the core socialist values and promote them throughout society.
A camp for future chemists BEIJING, May 4 230 senior undergraduates from 50 top Chinese universities captured a chance this summer to communicate with each other, announced Peking University College of Chemistry and Molecular Engineering (CCME), the organizer.
The 2014 Peking University Undergraduate Chemistry Summer Camp, the 7th since 2008, was held from July 4 to 6.
All the campers, divided into 7 groups by research major, participated in a comprehensive written test. Later in an interview, they presented their academic and research experience to the CCME professors, expressed their understanding of science and shared their dream and goals.

July 20, 2014

Welcome home, Mr. President!

Ethiopian President Mulatu Teshome paid a summer visit to Peking University, where he spent the whole college career of more than ten years.
“As an alumnus, I have always been proud of Peking University,” said Mulatu in his speech, “it is a great honor for me to be regarded by Peking University as her pride and joy as well.”
Together with his family, Mulatu was welcomed by Zhu Shanlu.
